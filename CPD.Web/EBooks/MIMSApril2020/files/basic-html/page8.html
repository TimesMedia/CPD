        Information for Prescribers


        When prescribing any product listed in MIMS, doctors should be mindful of the important points list ed below. Furthermore, it should be
        noted that these aspects will not necessarily appear in the product listing. Only where they are of clinical significance to the product
        have they been included.

                Hypersensitivity to the ingredient(s)=components of any medicine contra-indicates its use.

                Medicines should not be prescribed to pregnant or lactating women unless the antici pated benefit clearly outweighs any potential
               risk to the foetus.
                The possibility of drug accumulation should be considered in the presence of significant renal or hepatic impairment.

                Tolerance to medicines is likely to be less with extremes of age.

                Side effects refer only to those which occur at the recommended dosages and correct route of administration.

                Drug interferences with laboratory tests and physical incompatibilites common with paren teral solutions have not been included.

                Dosages given are for adults only unless otherwise stated. For additional information on dosage directions, particularly in the
               case of parenteral products, consult the MDR where applicable, the pharmaceutical company or the current package insert.

                All entries have been compiled strictly in accordance with current package processed by the pub lisher.

                For full prescribing information refer to the package insert approved by the Medicines Regulatory Authority.

                Mono amine oxidase inhibitors (MAOI) have a prolonged action, so patients should not take any of the foods or medicines known
               to cause reactions for at least 14 days after stopping treatment.

                Certain medicines (i.e. those that cause CNS depression) may lead to drowsiness and impaired concentration, which may
               be aggravated by the simultaneous intake of alcohol or other central nervous system depressant agents.
                Prices: MIMS reflects the single exit price at the rate of 14% inclusive of VAT (except when price prints in red indicating the
               adjustment to 15 %) in line with pricing legislation (Government Notice, Gazette No 26304 of 30 April 2004). Prescribers are reminded
               that this price excludes any professional fees that may be applic able and will not necessarily be what their patients will pay.

                NAPPI CODES: are unique nine or ten digit long product codes introduced to facilitate electronic  transactions. Each code is
               unique for product name, pack size, strength, manufacturer, etc.
               000000-000:   200 ml, R00,00.

                KEY TO USAGE IN SPORT             [P/S]   Permitted in sport
                                                  [PWR]   Permitted in sport with certain restrictions
                (For page reference to full expla na tory  [N/P/S]   Presentation not permitted in sport
                notes see "Contents page")        [N/P/S in C]  Presentation not permitted in competition

                When adverse reactions or side effects, e.g. fatigue, sedation, impaired psychomotor function and visual disturbances have been
               reported, patients should be advised not to engage in any activity which requires alert motor and other skills, such as driving,
               operating machinery or climbing dangerous heights until they know how the medication affects them, or are instructed by their
               treating clinician not to do so.
                With the reference lactose intolerance the prescriber is reminded that patients with rare hereditary problems of galactose
               intolerance, the Lapp lactase deficiency or glucose-galactose malabsorption should not take the medicine where lactose is
               part of the inactive ingredients or coating of the prescribed medicine.
